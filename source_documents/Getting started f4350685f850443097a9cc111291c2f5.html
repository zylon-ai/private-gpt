What is an architecture review?

It's a process where a team of reviewers examines a software system or application to ensure it's well-designed. They check things like security, performance, scalability, compliance, and cost optimization.

The goal is to identify areas for improvement and provide recommendations.

Why are we doing this?

Different incubations solve different problems and it would be interesting to share how we solve them.

We build systems, that we validate in the realm of our team, but we stay in that echo chamber, having other points of views can only help us grow.

Each team in their incubation is responsible for solving unique problems, and it can be interesting and valuable to share our approaches with each other.

While we validate our systems within our incubation team, this can lead to a limited range of perspectives.

By exploring different viewpoints, we can gain new insights and grow in our problem-solving abilities. It will also help us improve the quality of the products we build, and catch potential problems / issues early on.
